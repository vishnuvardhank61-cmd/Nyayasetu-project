Traffic Rights & e-Challan Guide

Navigate Indian roads with confidence. Know your rights during a stop, verify your digital documents, and manage challans legally.

1

Digital Validity

Digital copies in DigiLocker or mParivahan are legally equivalent to originals. Officers cannot deny them.

2

Key snatching is Illegal

No officer is permitted to snatch vehicle keys or manhandle drivers. It is a violation of your personal liberty.

3

Right to Identity

You have the right to ask for an officer's ID if they are not in uniform or don't have a visible name badge.

What to do if you are Pulled Over?

1. Stop Safely

Pull over to the side immediately without causing a hazard.

2. Be Polite

Acknowledge the officer. Keep your hands visible on the steering wheel/handle.

3. Show Docs

Provide your physical or digital DL and RC when asked.

4. Accept Challan

If you are wrong, accept the receipt. If not, note details to contest in Lok Adalat.

Pro Legal Tip: Lok Adalat

If you have multiple pending traffic challans or believe a challan was issued wrongly (e.g., technical error), do not rush to pay. Wait for the Traffic Lok Adalat held regularly in most cities. Magistrates there often reduce fines significantly or dismiss technical errors on the spot to clear backlog.

"Under Article 21, procedure established by law must be just, fair and reasonable. Arbitrary fines without evidence can be challenged."
